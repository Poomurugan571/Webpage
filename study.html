IIT Roorkee

The oldest technical institute in Asia established in 1847. It was named after the founder James Thomason as Thomason College of Civil Engineering. After the independence, it was renamed to University of Roorkee (UOR). Ever since its establishment, the quality of education and research outcome of this institute is outstanding. According to Institutes of Technology Act, 1961,  Indian government  considered UOR as Institutes of National Importance and gave the IIT status. Now IITR holds 387 in QS 2021 world ranking. so far IITR has produced 10 Padma awardees, 15  Shanti Swarup Bhatnagar Prize winners. Read more.

GATE

Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering (GATE) is an entrance exam conducted by the top 7 IITs on behalf of MHRD. Through GATE a graduate can get into IIT's/IISc and government Public sector Undertaking companies. GATE exam contains 65 questions out of which 15 are from general aptitude  and 50 questions from core subject. Each year only 15% of all appearing aspirants will qualify for the exam. Out of all qualified aspirants, only the top 99% scored aspirants will get an opportunity to get into PSU/IIT's. Which places the GATE exam stands 4th in the toughest exam in India. Read more.

Combustion

When fuel and oxygen mixture meets an ignition a luminous exothermic process occurs called a flame(combustion). The usage of combustion from getting protection from animals to nowaday a major source of energy the combustion became an important asset for the human  race to thrive. As the usage level of energy is being so high the importance of research in the field of combustion is very much needed. The area of combustion research is vast, few are premixed diffusion flame, solid propellant combustion, analyzing chemical kinematics, turbulent flames, spray combustion, detonation, instabilities, soot particle analysis, etc,  Read more.
